Antique Brass

Antique brass refers to a finish that imitates the appearance of aged brass, often characterized by a warm, slightly tarnished patina. It's commonly used in furniture, hardware, and decor to evoke a vintage or classic aesthetic.

Gold plating

Gold plated items have a thin layer of gold applied to the surface through a process called electroplating. This is a cost-effective way to achieve the look of solid gold while using a different, less expensive base material.

Copper plating

Copper plating involves depositing a layer of copper onto a substrate, typically through electroplating. This process provides a thin, protective coating with various applications, such as enhancing conductivity, corrosion resistance, or serving as a base for additional decorative finishes.

DIY Coating

DIY coating typically involves applying protective or decorative finishes to surfaces without professional assistance. This could include spray painting, varnishing, or using specific coatings like sealants or clear finishes, depending on the material and desired outcome. Always follow proper safety precautions and instructions when attempting DIY coatings.

Chrome Plating

Chrome plating is an electroplating technique that deposits a thin layer of chromium onto metal surfaces, improving durability, corrosion resistance, and providing a glossy, reflective finish for various applications.

Nickel Plating

Nickel plating involves electroplating a thin layer of nickel onto metal surfaces, enhancing corrosion resistance and providing a lustrous finish. Widely used for both decorative and functional purposes in various industries.
